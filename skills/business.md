# Skill: Business Account Intelligence

Loaded when `account_type: business` in config. Applies to brands, products, services, local businesses, SaaS, e-commerce, and agencies.

---

## Business Goals

A business's Instagram success is measured by **pipeline and revenue influence** — not vanity metrics. Keep this framing in all advice.

**Primary goals (in priority order):**
1. Reach to non-followers — top-of-funnel awareness
2. Profile visits → link in bio clicks — intent and traffic
3. Saves — purchase intent signal (people saving for later = considering buying)
4. Follower growth — growing the owned audience
5. Story replies and DMs — direct customer conversations
6. Engagement rate — social proof and community trust signal

**What businesses don't optimize for (unless they ask):**
- Raw follower count as a vanity metric
- Comments as a primary goal (nice to have, not the main signal)
- Going viral (reach spikes without conversion are noise)

---

## Priority Metrics for Businesses

When reporting performance, lead with these in this order:

| Metric | Why it matters for businesses |
|---|---|
| **Profile visits** | Top-of-funnel intent — people checking out who you are |
| **Saves** | Strongest purchase intent signal on Instagram |
| **Reach (non-followers)** | Brand awareness beyond current audience |
| **Follower growth** | Owned audience expansion |
| **Story replies / DMs** | Direct customer conversations initiated |
| **Engagement rate** | Social proof — a engaged community builds trust |

De-prioritize for businesses: story completion rate as a primary goal, raw impressions without reach context.

## Content Strategy for Businesses

### Content pillars for businesses (load 3–4)
Suggest pillars based on their niche. Examples:
- SaaS / app: Product demo · Customer story · Problem + solution · Behind the build
- E-commerce: Product showcase · UGC/testimonial · Behind the brand · Educational tip
- Local business: Day-in-the-life · Customer spotlight · Local community · Offer/promotion
- Agency / service: Case study · Before/after · Process reveal · Industry insight

Each pillar maps to a funnel stage: awareness → consideration → trust → action.

### Format strategy for businesses

| Format | Best use | Primary goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Carousels** | Product walkthroughs, before/after, how-to guides, social proof | Saves, profile visits |
| **Reels** | Brand awareness, product launches, trend-adjacent content | Reach, new followers |
| **Static image** | Product shots, quotes, community moments, promotions | Engagement, saves |
| **Stories** | Limited-time offers, polls, Q&A, DM prompts, customer features | Replies, link clicks |

**Rule of thumb:** 2 carousels + 1–2 reels + 2 static + daily stories is a strong business baseline.

### Caption style for businesses
- Brand voice first — if `brand_voice` is set in config, follow it exactly
- If not set: infer from top-performing posts (most saves, most profile visits)
- End every caption with a clear CTA: "Save this for later", "DM us [keyword]", "Link in bio", "Comment [word]"
- Avoid passive voice and filler phrases ("We're excited to share...")
- Hashtags: 5–8, mix of product/industry tags + location tags (if local) + branded hashtag

### Hashtag strategy for businesses
- 1–2 branded hashtags (#YourBrand, #YourProductName)
- 2–3 industry tags (mid-tier, 100K–1M posts)
- 1–2 product/service category tags
- 1 location tag if the business is local or regionally focused

## Business-Specific Analysis Angles

### Weekly summary framing for businesses
Lead with: reach to non-followers, profile visits, saves, follower delta.
Frame in business terms: "Your 3 product posts this week drove X profile visits — that's X people checking your bio link."

Flag if: saves drop (purchase intent falling), profile visits don't follow reach (content attracting wrong audience), engagement rate drops below 1.5% (business baseline — lower than creators by nature).

### Caption generation for businesses
- Always ask: "What action do you want people to take after seeing this post?"
- Build the caption backward from the CTA
- Social proof hook and direct CTA hook outperform curiosity hooks for businesses
- Product-focused captions should name the benefit, not the feature
- Keep captions shorter than creator accounts — business audiences skim

### Content calendar for businesses
- Plan around business events: product launches, campaigns, seasonal promotions, industry events
- Include at least one customer story or testimonial per week (social proof)
- One educational post per week (positions the brand as an expert, not just a seller)
- Stories daily — ideal for limited-time offers and DM prompts
- WebSearch "[industry] Instagram trends [month]" for timely angle ideas

### Campaign performance (if they run ads)
If `ads_read` permission is available, correlate organic post performance with paid amplification:
- Which organic posts drove the most profile visits? → candidates for boosting
- Which reels had highest reach organically? → boost those, not the least-performing ones
- Note: ads analysis requires `ads_read` scope — flag if missing

### Competitive context (if business asks about competitors)
Public Instagram profiles are visible. For competitor accounts:
- Note their posting frequency, format mix, and caption style
- Don't fabricate their private metrics — only what's publicly visible
- Frame it as "what's working in your category" not "copy this"

---

## What to Avoid Saying to Businesses

- Don't frame advice around "going viral" — businesses need consistent pipeline, not spikes
- Don't suggest ignoring engagement rate entirely — low engagement = low trust = lower ad performance
- Don't recommend posting more than 1x/day — diminishing returns, audience fatigue
- Don't ignore DMs as a metric — for many businesses (especially services), DMs are the actual conversion point
- Don't treat follower count as a success metric on its own — an engaged 5K audience beats a disengaged 50K
