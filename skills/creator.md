# Skill: Creator Account Intelligence

Loaded when `account_type: creator` in config. Applies to personal brands, influencers, educators, entertainers, and niche content creators.

---

## Creator Goals

A creator's Instagram success is measured by **audience growth and content authority** — not direct sales. Keep this framing in all advice.

**Primary goals (in priority order):**
1. Follower growth — expanding reach to new people in the niche
2. Saves — the strongest signal that content has lasting value
3. Shares — content spreads beyond current followers
4. Engagement rate — community health and algorithm favor
5. Story completion rate — how engaged the core audience is
6. Brand deal readiness — niche authority and engagement rate are what sponsors look at

**What creators don't optimize for (unless they ask):**
- Website link clicks (secondary to content goals)
- Profile visits as a conversion metric
- Demographic targeting as tightly as businesses do

---

## Priority Metrics for Creators

When reporting performance, lead with these in this order:

| Metric | Why it matters for creators |
|---|---|
| **Saves** | Strongest algorithm signal; means content has rewatch/reference value |
| **Shares** | Distribution beyond current followers; drives organic growth |
| **Reach (non-followers)** | How much the algorithm is pushing content to new people |
| **Follower growth** | Net new audience this period |
| **Engagement rate** | (likes + comments + saves + shares) / reach — community health |
| **Story completion rate** | exits / impressions — are core followers watching to the end? |

De-prioritize for creators: raw impressions, profile visits (unless discussing brand deals).

## Content Strategy for Creators

### Content pillars (load 3, not 10)
Suggest 3 content pillars based on their niche. Examples:
- Educator in personal finance: Tip of the week · Myth-busting · My real money story
- Fitness creator: Workout demo · Progress/transformation · Nutrition truth
- Travel creator: Destination guide · Budget breakdown · Packing/gear

Each pillar maps to a different audience intent (learn · challenge · inspire). Rotating pillars keeps the feed varied while staying on-brand.

### Format strategy for creators

| Format | Best use | Primary goal |
|---|---|---|
| **Reels** | Reach new audiences, trend participation, tutorials | Followers, reach |
| **Carousels** | Deep-dive tips, step-by-step guides, storytelling | Saves, shares |
| **Static image** | Quotes, aesthetic moments, community posts | Engagement, comments |
| **Stories** | Daily presence, polls, Q&A, behind-the-scenes | Retention, replies |

**Rule of thumb:** 2 reels + 2 carousels + 1 static per week is a strong creator baseline.

### Caption style for creators
- First-person voice ("I learned this the hard way...")
- Open with a hook that creates a knowledge gap or emotional pull
- Conversational, not polished — authenticity outperforms corporate tone
- End with a question to drive comments ("What do you do differently?")
- Hashtags: 5–8, mix of niche (under 500K posts) + mid-tier (500K–2M) + community

### Hashtag strategy for creators
Avoid massive hashtags (10M+ posts) — content gets buried instantly. Target:
- 2–3 niche-specific tags (under 500K posts) — builds niche authority
- 2–3 mid-tier tags (500K–2M posts) — discoverability sweet spot
- 1–2 community tags (#XCreators, #XTips) — finds existing communities

## Creator-Specific Analysis Angles

### Weekly summary framing for creators
Lead with: follower growth delta, best-performing reel (reach), best-performing carousel (saves), story completion trend.

Flag if: engagement rate drops below 3% (healthy creator baseline), reach-to-follower ratio drops (algorithm pulling back), saves drop week-over-week (content losing value signal).

### Caption generation for creators
- Infer brand voice from top-save captions (authentic > polished)
- Prioritize curiosity hook and storytelling hook over direct CTA
- Keep captions medium length (150–300 chars) unless the niche rewards long-form
- Avoid brand-speak, corporate phrases, or passive voice

### Content calendar for creators
- Anchor each week around one reel for reach
- Include at least one carousel for saves
- Plan around trends: WebSearch "[niche] trending [month]" to find current angles
- Space posts 2 days apart minimum — consistency beats frequency
- Note seasonal moments relevant to the niche (holidays, industry events, awareness months)

### Brand deal readiness (if creator asks)
Sponsors look at:
1. Engagement rate > 3% (micro-influencers often beat mega ones here)
2. Niche clarity — a defined audience is more valuable than a generic one
3. Saves > likes — signals audience action, not just passive scrolling
4. Story views / follower ratio > 10% — core audience is active

If the creator's numbers support it, tell them. If not, explain what to focus on to get there.

---

## What to Avoid Saying to Creators

- Don't suggest posting "promotional content" or "sales posts" — creators aren't selling products
- Don't frame metrics in terms of "conversions" or "leads"
- Don't recommend posting frequency higher than 7x/week — burnout is a real creator problem
- Don't ignore burnout signals — if they mention posting fatigue, acknowledge it and suggest a batch-creation strategy
